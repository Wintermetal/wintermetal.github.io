Backstory

The "Mighty Hood", as she was known, was built by John Brown & Company Shipyards, Clydebank, Scotland. During her 21 year long career, she, more than any other ship, would stand as the ultimate symbol of the Empire's might. Of all the vessels to bear the name, she was the most important, most memorable and most loved. She was sunk in the Battle of the Denmark strait in 24 of August 1941. It is unknown when exactly her ghost started to appear, but the first suspected appearance was in 1967 and the ghost of HMS Hood was spotted by a fishing boat on the North Atlantic as the crew reported seeing an old world war 2 era battleship. After that she has been sighted multiple times sailing in different parts of the world. Most of the time she has been seen near the Denmark strait and other places she patrolled before sinking, but she has been seen in other places too.
